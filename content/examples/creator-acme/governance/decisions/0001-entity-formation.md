# C-Corp Formation in Delaware

## Problem Statement

Creator Acme needs a legal entity to sign sponsorship contracts, run payroll, and own intellectual property. The choice of jurisdiction affects tax treatment and the cost of any future capital raise.

## Considered Options

LLC in Delaware (lower setup cost, pass-through taxation), LLC in California (operator's home state), C-Corp in Delaware (default for any future venture-backed path).

## Decision

Form a Delaware C-Corp. The cost premium over an LLC is modest, and the optionality on future capital raises (institutional sponsorships, cohort acquisition, audience-platform M&A) is worth the predictable structure.

## Reasoning

The creator-business path frequently crosses into structured capital — sponsorship pre-payments, cohort enterprise contracts, equity-share arrangements with sub-creators — which a Delaware C-Corp accommodates without restructuring. Founder is the sole shareholder for the foreseeable future.
