# Founder-Held Sponsor-Fit Veto and Two-Active-Deals Cap

## Problem Statement

The fastest path to revenue in the creator-business shape is sponsorship volume. Sponsorship volume erodes audience trust faster than any other lever the team controls. The team needs a written rule for how sponsorship decisions are made and who holds the veto.

## Considered Options

Open pipeline with operations team approving deals on margin, sponsor-fit veto held by the founder with a hard cap on concurrent deals, no sponsorships at all (membership and cohort revenue only).

## Decision

Founder holds the sponsor-fit veto on every deal regardless of size. No more than two sponsorship deals are active at any given time. Operations lead screens inbound, founder decides go/no-go, and any deal that misses the brand-fit bar is declined even if the operations team has invested time in negotiation.

## Reasoning

The audience Creator Acme builds is the audience that buys cohort seats and memberships at $1,750 and $9/month respectively — both monetisation channels are an order of magnitude more dependent on editorial trust than on sponsorship pipeline volume. A two-deal cap keeps the surface area manageable and reduces the chance that the audience experiences sponsorship saturation. Founder veto is the single point of editorial discipline; if delegated, the bar erodes by predictable amounts every quarter. Re-evaluate annually.
