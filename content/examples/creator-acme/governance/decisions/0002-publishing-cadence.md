# One Long-Form Per Week, One Livestream Per Month

## Problem Statement

The dominant pattern in operator-content monetisation is volume: more shorts, more clips, more daily posts. Volume earns short-term reach and degrades long-term audience quality. The decision is what cadence Creator Acme commits to, and which formats are out of scope.

## Considered Options

Daily shorts plus weekly long-form (high reach, high attention cost), three long-forms per week (volume play, sustainable for at most six months), one long-form per week plus one monthly livestream (sustainable indefinitely; sets the bar for editorial quality).

## Decision

One long-form deep-dive every Wednesday. One livestream AMA per month. Channel-trailer style shorts allowed for episode promotion only. No daily shorts, no daily commentary, no algorithm-led format experiments.

## Reasoning

The audience Creator Acme is building is the audience that pays for cohorts and memberships — they convert on editorial trust, not on volume. Editorial trust is set by the bar of each individual deep-dive; the bar drops if the cadence demands more output than the team can sustain. The monthly livestream provides community access without compromising the long-form bar. Re-evaluate at the 18-month mark or if monthly subscriber growth drops below 1,000.
