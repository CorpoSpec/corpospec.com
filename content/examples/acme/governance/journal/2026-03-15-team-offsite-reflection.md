# Team Offsite Reflection

**Date:** 2026-03-15

## Context

First full-team offsite since founding. 12 people in San Francisco for 3 days. Mix of strategy sessions, team building, and product planning.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Culture is solid.** Remote-first works well for this team size. The offsite confirmed that async communication practices (Linear, Notion, Slack) are effective. No misalignment between sub-teams.

2. **Engineering velocity is high.** Sarah's team shipped 47 PRs last sprint. The monolith-first architecture decision (BDR 0003) is paying off. No microservice overhead. Deploy time is under 4 minutes.

3. **Product-market fit signal is strong.** Customer interviews during the offsite validated that the AI workflow templates are the primary differentiator. Competitors have generic automation. Our templates learn from usage patterns. This is defensible.

4. **Series A readiness.** Jordan (independent board member) joined for the strategy session. His feedback: the metrics are strong enough for Series A conversations to begin in Q3. Target $10-15M raise at $80-120M pre-money. He will make introductions to Sequoia, a16z, and Lightspeed.

5. **Hiring priorities post-Series A.** Consensus on first three hires: Head of Sales, Customer Success Manager, Senior Backend Engineer. Engineering-first hiring plan (BDR 0006) shifts to include go-to-market roles.
